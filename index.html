What is CMTAT

An evolving open standard
not a product or static ERC.

CMTAT — the CMTA Token — is an open smart contract standard that defines everything a security token needs to be compliant, programmable, and interoperable.

CMTAT is already integrated in the major banking wallet providers, including Taurus and Fireblocks.

CMTAT was designed collaboratively by banks, lawyers, custodians, and Web3 technologists. It is maintained by the CMTA, with key contributions from Taurus and UBS.

The reference Solidity implementation is battle-tested and audited, ready to deploy on Ethereum and any EVM chain including Avalanche, Polygon, and Arbitrum.

🏛️ Not tied to any single vendor
Governed by CMTA and its Technological Committee, with contribution from key players in law, finance, and Web3 technology.
🔧 Modular by architecture
Add or remove modules to match your functional and regulatory requirements. Need debt instrument support? Add the module.
⚖️ Jurisdiction-agnostic
CMTAT is used in Germany, Singapore, Switzerland, Turkey, UAE, USA, and more.
Capabilities

Every feature you need
for Web3 adoption, compliance, and security.

Every CMTAT feature is purpose-built for regulated tokenization use cases, leveraging industry standards and modern approaches.

🔒

Conditional Transfers

Token transfers can be gated by an external, customizable Rule Engine. Whitelist addresses, enforce sanctions screening, set jurisdiction limits.

ERC-1404
🧊

Account Freeze & Token Pause

Freeze individual wallets (partial or total) or pause all transfers globally. Burn and mint remain available even during a pause, enabling corporate actions without lockdown.

Compliance
📈

Mint, Burn & Forced Transfer

Full lifecycle management: issue new shares, redeem, execute forced transfers for corporate actions and compliance enforcement.

Lifecycle
⛽

Gasless Transactions (Meta-Tx)

Investors don't need ETH to transact. ERC-2771 support via OpenGSN allows issuers or platforms to sponsor gas fees.

ERC-2771
🔄

Upgradeability

Deploy with a proxy pattern (Transparent or UUPS) to upgrade the token's logic without changing its address. ERC-7201 storage namespacing prevents collisions between modules.

ERC-7201
🌉

Cross-Chain Transfer

ERC-7802 support enables native cross-chain bridging. CMTAT is even compatible with Chainlink CCIP and LayerZero's cross-chain technologies.

ERC-7802
📄

Document Management

Attach legal documents (prospectus, terms, corporate actions) directly on-chain via ERC-1643. Every document is hashed and timestamped, providing an immutable audit trail.

ERC-1643
🔑

Key Loss Recovery

Token holders don't lose their assets if they lose their private key. When registered holders exist off-chain, the issuer can recover assets to a new wallet.

Investor Protection
🎯

Batch Operations

Execute multiple mints, burns, or administrative actions in a single transaction. Critical for large investor bases and corporate events like dividend distributions or cap table migrations.

Stablecoins

The framework powering
live regulated stablecoins.

Live in Production — UAE, November 2025

CMTAT is not just a security token framework. Iit handles regulated stablecoins with the same architecture. The compliance modules that enforce investor eligibility on equity tokens work just as well for mint/burn controls, reserve transparency, and regulatory reporting on fiat-backed digital currencies.

In November 2025, Zand Bank, licensed by the Central Bank of the UAE, launched the country's first regulated, multi-chain AED-backed stablecoin on public blockchains.

The stablecoin is fully backed one-to-one by AED reserves in segregated, regulated accounts, available across multiple public blockchains for borderless settlement. It marks a new category of regulated stablecoin: institutionally issued, publicly verifiable, built on an open standard that any developer can inspect and audit.

Why CMTAT for Stablecoins

💡 No Proprietary Identity Required

Unlike other standards, CMTAT doesn't force you to adopt an on-chain identity system. For a stablecoin issuer, your KYC is handled off-chain. CMTAT's optional RuleEngine plugs into whatever compliance infrastructure you already have.

🔄 Full Lifecycle, Out of the Box

Mint on issuance. Burn on redemption. Freeze on sanctions flags. Pause on regulatory action. All standard CMTAT features — no custom development needed for core stablecoin operations.

🌐 Multi-Chain from Day One

ERC-7802 cross-chain support is native. Deploy the same stablecoin contract across Ethereum, Avalanche, Polygon, and other EVM chains — one codebase, consistent compliance logic, centralized lifecycle management.

Privacy & Confidentiality

Tokens that keep
institutional secrets.

Public blockchains are transparent by default, which is precisely what criminals love and institutions fear. CMTAT addresses this head-on with a confidential variant and a proof of concept on a ZK-native L2.

🔒

Confidential Token (Zama) support

A variant of CMTAT implements the Confidential Token interface to hide balances and transfer amounts, while preserving all the compliance controls that make CMTAT suitable for regulated instruments.

It retains the modularity of the standard CMTAT, meaning issuers can adopt confidentiality selectively: only the modules that require privacy carry the cryptographic overhead. The rest of the lifecycle (mint, burn, freeze, pause) is unchanged.

github.com/CMTA/CMTAT-Confidential →
🌀

Private token for the Aztec L2

In a proof of concept with Aztec — an Ethereum Layer-2 built on ZK cryptography at the protocol level — CMTAT demonstrated fully private settlement of both a confidential security token and a confidential stablecoin.

The PoC achieved delivery-versus-payment (DvP) settlement where transaction amounts, participant identities, and balances are encrypted on-chain and invisible to third parties, except for authorized parties.
